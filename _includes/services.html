About the DEMON Project

In a variegated ecosystem of online news, opinions and facts, we move away from a traditional and established research setting – which focuses on automatically classifying pieces of content that can be only either true or false – and consider the problem of characterizing how the variety of information shared through online channels can be manipulated from both a content and social network perspective. To study information manipulation we plan to adapt and extend techniques from NLP (to automatically process text) and data/network science (to study information diffusion in online social networks), such as to eventually detect and evaluate the extent to which specific stories – originated by news outlets, government bodies and other public influential figures – are being exploited or weaponized by (malicious) actors in the online information ecosystem, as opposed to more organic patterns of sharing and interactions. The resulting framework will pave the way to a better understanding of the broader implications of online information in the real world, allowing to face situations where the spreading of potentially harmful information can be critical.

Data Science

Data collection, clustering of stories and categorization of sub-narratives.

Natural Language Processing

Content-based analysis of manipulation of sub-narratives.

Social Network Analysis

Network-based analysis of manipulation of sub-narratives.
